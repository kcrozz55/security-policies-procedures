## Overview

This repository contains the complete Information Security Policy Library for MedCore Health Systems. All policies govern the protection of information assets, electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI), and the systems that process, store, and transmit that information across the organization.

These policies apply to all MedCore employees, contractors, business associates, and any third parties granted access to MedCore systems or data. Compliance with these policies is mandatory and subject to enforcement under MedCore's Human Resources Disciplinary Policy and applicable law.

## Policy Structure

Each policy in this library follows the MedCore Standard Policy Template and contains:

1. **Purpose** — Why the policy exists and what it aims to protect
2. **Scope** — Who and what the policy applies to
3. **Policy Statement** — The specific requirements and rules
4. **Roles and Responsibilities** — Who is responsible for compliance and enforcement
5. **Compliance and Enforcement** — Consequences for violations
6. **Exceptions** — Process for requesting and approving exceptions
7. **Related Documents** — Cross-references to related policies and procedures
8. **Revision History** — Version tracking and change log

## Policy Governance

### Policy Development and Review Process

All MedCore information security policies follow the Policy Development Lifecycle:

1. **Initiation** — CISO or ISSO identifies need for new or revised policy
2. **Drafting** — Policy owner drafts policy following standard template
3. **Review** — Legal, HR, Privacy Officer, and affected department heads review
4. **Approval** — CISO approves; CEO endorsement for organization-wide policies
5. **Communication** — Policy published; affected parties notified; training updated
6. **Implementation** — Policy operationalized through procedures and controls
7. **Monitoring** — Compliance monitored through audits and ConMon program
8. **Review and Update** — Annual review cycle or triggered by significant change

### Policy Exception Process

Requests to deviate from any policy requirement must be submitted to the ISSO using the Policy Exception Request Form (MedCore-FORM-POL-001). All exception requests must include:
- The specific policy requirement for which an exception is requested
- Business justification for the exception
- Risk assessment of granting the exception
- Proposed compensating controls
- Requested duration of the exception

Exception requests are reviewed by the ISSO and approved by the CISO. Exceptions exceeding 90 days require AO awareness. All approved exceptions are tracked in the Policy Exception Register.

| ISO/IEC 27001:2013 | Voluntary alignment | Annex A controls |
