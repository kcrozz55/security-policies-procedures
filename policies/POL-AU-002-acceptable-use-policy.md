## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) establishes the requirements and restrictions governing the use of MedCore Health Systems' information technology resources, including computer systems, networks, software applications, electronic communications, and data. The purpose of this policy is to protect MedCore's information assets, ensure compliance with applicable laws and regulations (including HIPAA), and maintain the security, integrity, and availability of systems used to support patient care.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all individuals who access MedCore information technology resources, including:
- Full-time and part-time employees
- Contractors, consultants, and temporary staff
- Business associates and vendors with system access
- Students, interns, and volunteers
- Any third party accessing MedCore systems under a formal agreement

This policy applies to all MedCore-owned, leased, or managed systems, as well as personally owned devices used to access MedCore resources (Bring Your Own Device — BYOD).

---

## 3. Policy Statement

### 3.1 Authorized Use

MedCore information technology resources are provided for conducting MedCore business and supporting the delivery of patient care. Users are granted access based on their role and the principle of least privilege. All access is subject to monitoring and auditing.

### 3.2 User Responsibilities

All users of MedCore IT resources must:

- Use systems only for authorized MedCore business purposes
- Protect their credentials — never share usernames, passwords, or MFA tokens
- Lock workstations when stepping away, even briefly
- Report suspected security incidents or policy violations to the ISSO immediately
- Complete all required security awareness training within designated timeframes
- Comply with all applicable MedCore policies and applicable laws
- Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI at all times

### 3.3 Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited on MedCore IT resources:

**Data and Privacy Violations:**
- Accessing, copying, or transmitting ePHI or PII beyond what is required for job duties
- Sharing patient information via personal email accounts, personal cloud storage, or unauthorized messaging platforms
- Taking photographs or screenshots of patient data for personal purposes
- Discussing patient information in public areas or via unencrypted channels

**Security Violations:**
- Attempting to bypass, disable, or circumvent any security control
- Installing unauthorized software, applications, or browser extensions
- Connecting unauthorized hardware (USB drives, external hard drives) without IT approval
- Attempting to access systems, data, or areas for which you are not authorized
- Using another person's credentials or allowing others to use yours
- Introducing malware, viruses, or malicious code of any kind
- Conducting unauthorized network scans, penetration testing, or vulnerability assessments

**Inappropriate Use:**
- Using MedCore systems for personal financial gain or outside business activities
- Accessing, downloading, or distributing obscene, harassing, or discriminatory content
- Engaging in activities that violate federal, state, or local law
- Excessive personal use that interferes with job performance or consumes significant network resources
- Sending unsolicited bulk email (spam) using MedCore systems

**Remote and Mobile Use:**
- Connecting to MedCore systems via public Wi-Fi without using the MedCore VPN
- Leaving MedCore devices unattended in vehicles or unsecured public locations
- Allowing family members or others to use MedCore-issued devices or access credentials
- Storing ePHI on personally owned devices without authorization and approved encryption

### 3.4 Email and Electronic Communications

MedCore-provided email is the authorized channel for business communications. Users must:
- Not use personal email accounts for MedCore business involving ePHI or sensitive information
- Verify recipient addresses before sending sensitive information
- Not click links or open attachments from unknown or suspicious senders
- Report phishing attempts immediately to the Security Operations Center (SOC)
- Understand that email sent from or received on MedCore systems is not private and may be monitored

### 3.5 Internet Use

Internet access is provided to support job functions. Users must:
- Not visit websites known to distribute malware, illegal content, or content prohibited under this policy
- Not use web-based proxies or anonymizing tools to circumvent MedCore web filtering
- Not download software, games, or media for personal use
- Exercise caution when sharing any MedCore or patient information on social media platforms

### 3.6 Social Media

Employees using personal social media accounts must not:
- Post patient information, photos, or any identifying health information
- Represent personal opinions as MedCore's official position
- Post confidential business information about MedCore operations, financial status, or security practices
- Disparage patients, colleagues, or MedCore in ways that may harm the organization's reputation

---

## 4. Monitoring and Auditing

Users have no expectation of privacy when using MedCore IT resources. MedCore reserves the right to monitor, access, review, audit, and disclose all information created, transmitted, received, or stored on MedCore systems, in accordance with applicable law. Monitoring may include but is not limited to:
- Network traffic analysis
- Email content review
- Web browsing activity
- Login and authentication records
- Workstation activity logs
- Access to ePHI and other sensitive data

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| All Users | Comply with this policy; report violations |
| Managers / Supervisors | Ensure direct reports complete training; report violations |
| ISSO | Monitor compliance; investigate violations; maintain policy |
| CISO | Approve policy; set enforcement standards; review exceptions |
| IT Helpdesk | Assist users with compliant use; report suspected violations to ISSO |
| Human Resources | Support disciplinary process; maintain records of policy acknowledgment |

---

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

All users are required to read and acknowledge this policy upon onboarding and annually thereafter. Acknowledgment records are maintained by Human Resources.

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract, as well as referral to law enforcement if criminal activity is suspected. Violations involving ePHI may additionally trigger HIPAA breach assessment procedures and regulatory reporting obligations.

---

## 7. Exceptions

Requests for exceptions to this policy must be submitted to the ISSO using the Policy Exception Request Form (MedCore-FORM-POL-001) and approved by the CISO prior to engaging in any activity that would otherwise violate this policy.
